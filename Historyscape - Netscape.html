Netscape 0.9 to 9


Ah, Netscape...
The once dominant web browser that got bought by AOL and lost its status as the browser...

History
Netscape was released in 1994 as a continuation of the NCSA Mosaic browser released a year prior, which was revolutionary as it could load images and text at the same time.
Around 1995, problems started emerging when Internet Explorer (which camed pre-installed on Windows) was starting to gain in popularity... This was the dawn of the first Browser War, which ended in 2001. By the time Netscape 4 released, Netscape was starting to scramble. They were losing marketshare, and fast. As an attempt to make a successor, Netscape released its source code in 1998, which would spawn Mozilla Suite, which had public releases from 1999 until the final version of Mozilla released in 2003. It also spawned Firefox in 2001, originally as an experimental browser, but proved to become popular. Another thing that happened in 1998 was the acquisition of Netscape Communications, in which they got bought by AOL. In 2000, Netscape had lost a ton of its userbase, and ended up using an unfinished build of Mozilla as its base with lots of bloatware added on... That release was Netscape 6, which was a horridly buggy mess, and while versions 6.1 to 7 improved on it in many ways, the original N6 release hurt the browser's reputation, spiraling it down even more... In 2005, AOL got Mercurial Communications to develop Netscape 8, which was based off of both the Firefox and Internet Explore architecture, as it has a unique feature to switch engines. Netscape 9 was the final release, and was developed by AOL again. It was based off of Firefox 2.0, and by the time this release came out, many of its Netscape-y characteristics had gone...

I'm not sure why, but I find the evolution of Netscape (Particularly Version 6) quite interesting. Maybe it's just me liking old things...
